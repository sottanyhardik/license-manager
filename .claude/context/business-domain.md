# Context — Business Domain

Orientation only. Authoritative: `docs/01-project-overview.md`, `docs/03-features.md`,
`docs/06-business-rules.md`, `docs/guides/COMMISSION_ACCOUNTING_GUIDE.md`.

## The domain in five terms

- **License** — a DGFT entitlement (DFIA in USD CIF, or incentive: RODTEP/ROSTL/MEIS in INR)
  earned against export performance. Consumed over time; balance is per license, per company.
- **Allotment** — a pre-authorisation that *reserves* part of a license balance for a specific
  importer before customs clearance.
- **Bill of Entry (BOE)** — an actual customs clearance that *debits* the license. Rows become
  `frozen` (locked) after ledger upload.
- **Transfer** — the license (or part) changes hands; requires a formal DGFT transfer letter
  (generated by the app).
- **Trade invoice** — sale/purchase transactions that must reconcile against BOEs and allotments
  (commission accounting applies — see the guide above).

## Invariants an AI must not break

- A license balance cannot be over-drawn; debits net against reservations and clearances.
- Balances are **materialised** and maintained by signals — change them through the recalc path.
- `frozen` BOE rows are immutable.
- Master data (companies, ports, HS/SION norms, exchange rates) is owned by the canonical
  server and synced one-way; treat it as authoritative reference data.
